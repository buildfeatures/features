🤖 Feature Overview

What is Fraud Heatmap Overlay?

Fraud Heatmap Overlay is an AI-assisted investigation feature that transforms long screen-recording evidence into an interactive visual timeline. Instead of manually reviewing every second of a video, the system continuously analyzes behavioral signals such as cursor movement, typing rhythm, idle periods, window switching, click patterns, scrolling behavior, and interaction consistency to calculate a dynamic suspicion score throughout the recording. The generated heatmap allows reviewers to instantly navigate to high-risk moments, understand why suspicious behavior was detected, and complete investigations significantly faster while maintaining human oversight over the final decision.



Why It Exists


Manual review of proof videos is slow, repetitive, and often inconsistent. Investigators frequently spend most of their time watching normal behavior before finding a few seconds of suspicious activity. Fraud Heatmap Overlay reduces investigation time by using AI to continuously analyze behavioral patterns and visualize suspicious moments directly on the video timeline, allowing reviewers to focus only on events that require attention.

⚙️ How It Works

How Fraud Heatmap Overlay Works

Fraud Heatmap Overlay continuously analyzes screen recordings from beginning to end, extracts behavioral signals, evaluates each interaction using AI models, generates dynamic suspicion scores, and visualizes high-risk moments through an interactive heatmap. Instead of reviewing an entire recording manually, investigators can immediately jump to the most suspicious events while understanding why each event was flagged.

Workflow Explanation


The system first processes every frame of the uploaded screen recording while collecting behavioral signals such as cursor movement, typing speed, click frequency, idle periods, scrolling activity, and application switching. These signals are normalized and passed to an AI scoring engine that continuously evaluates user behavior throughout the recording.


Instead of producing only a final fraud score, the AI assigns confidence values to every important moment in the recording. Those values are transformed into a visual heatmap where higher suspicion levels appear as more prominent hotspots along the video timeline.


Investigators simply click any highlighted section to jump directly to the relevant evidence, review AI explanations, and make the final decision based on both automated analysis and human judgment.
